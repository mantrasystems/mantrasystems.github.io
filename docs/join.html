Our Medical Affairs Training Program allows doctors to learn how to perform contractor-based medical affairs work

The program

This is a unique opportunity to learn more about the medical device industry, both in terms of developing skills and understanding what a substantive role might involve. It's also highly attractive to recruiters if a full-time role is pursued within the industry.

Paid work is on a contractor-contractee relationship so there is no ongoing commitment but with the potential to significantly enhance income.

The work would be in relation to the Medical Device Regulation 2017/745, performing specialist tasks that involve the analysis and appraisal of clinical data in very specific ways under our framework.

Training stages

  1. Pre-course work introduces the EU MDR 2017/745 to give a background into the core aspects of Medical Device Regulation.
  2. Session 1 webinar covers...
  3. Session 2 webinar covers...
  4. Post-course work requires the completion of a mock Clinical Evaluation Report within a set time period. This is then assessed by a member of our medical team with additional feedback sessions.
  5. Following satisfactory completion, doctors are able to undertake work immediately.
